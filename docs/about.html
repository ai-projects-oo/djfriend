About DJFriend

Your AI-powered set planning tool

DJFriend takes your music library and turns it into a perfectly structured DJ set — harmonically mixed, energy-shaped, and export-ready.

What it is

Built for DJs, by a DJ

DJFriend is a desktop app (Mac & Windows) and web tool that analyses your music library and automatically builds DJ sets that flow. It combines real audio analysis — BPM, musical key, energy — with AI-powered semantic tagging to select tracks that work together harmonically and emotionally.

Think of it as the smart layer between your music collection and your DJ software. You describe the vibe, draw the energy curve, and DJFriend picks the tracks.

How it works

Step by step

1

Load your library

Import your music from a local folder, Apple Music playlist, Rekordbox XML, or drop in an M3U / TXT playlist file. DJFriend reads the audio files directly — no uploads, your music stays on your computer.

2

Analysis runs automatically

For each track, DJFriend extracts BPM, musical key (Camelot wheel), and energy level from the audio. It also fetches genres from Spotify and uses AI (Groq) to add semantic tags — vibe, mood, venue fit, and time-of-night.

3

Shape your energy curve

Use the curve editor to draw the energy arc of your set — a slow warm-up, a peak-time surge, a gradual outro. Choose from 5 presets or draw it freehand. Set your target duration (30–180 min) and BPM range.

4

Generate the set

Hit Generate. DJFriend scores every track against the next using harmonic compatibility (Camelot wheel), BPM continuity, energy match, and semantic affinity. It builds the optimal sequence automatically. You can optionally restrict generation to a specific imported playlist using Source mode.

5

Refine and export

Each track shows a fit indicator (hover for details on harmonic clashes, energy gaps, or BPM jumps). Swap any track and get up to 5 smart replacement suggestions scored by harmonic, energy, and BPM fit. Reorder via drag-and-drop, then export as M3U or send directly to Spotify.

Import sources

Works with your existing workflow

Local Folder

Point DJFriend at any folder of audio files. MP3, FLAC, AAC, WAV, M4A, OGG supported.

Apple Music

Select any playlist from your Apple Music library directly on Mac. No export needed.

Rekordbox XML

Export your Rekordbox collection as XML and import it — BPM and key data carry over.

AI features

What the AI adds

DJFriend uses Groq (free, fast) to enrich every track with tags that go beyond raw audio data:

Vibe — driving · hypnotic · dreamy · groovy Mood — dark · uplifting · euphoric · melancholic Venue — club · festival · lounge · warehouse Time of night — warm-up · peak-time · after-hours Vocal type — vocal · instrumental · mostly-vocal Per-track fit indicator — harmonic, energy & BPM signals Smart swap — top 5 replacements scored by fit Generate from playlist — restrict to any imported playlist

These tags are used as a scoring bonus when selecting tracks, so the set not only sounds right harmonically — it also feels right emotionally. The fit indicator and smart swap surface problems and solutions inline, so you can refine the set without leaving the app.

FAQ

Common questions

Does DJFriend upload my music anywhere?

No. Audio analysis happens locally on your computer. The only external calls are to Spotify (for genres) and Groq (for semantic tags) — track metadata only, never audio.

Do I need a Spotify account?

Spotify is used to fetch genre data for tracks. You need a Spotify Developer app (free) with a Client ID and Secret. Contact us and we'll help you set it up.

Do I need a Groq API key?

Only for AI semantic tagging. Groq is free — sign up at console.groq.com, generate a key, and paste it into DJFriend Settings. Without it, the app still works using audio analysis alone.

Can I use it without the desktop app?

Yes — the web version at djfriend.onrender.com lets you import playlists by file (M3U, TXT) and get AI-estimated BPM, key, and energy without any audio files. Request access via email.

What DJ software does the export work with?

The M3U export opens in Rekordbox, Serato, VirtualDJ, Traktor, and any other software that reads M3U playlists. The Spotify export creates a playlist directly in your account.
